* Echoes of Dystopia *

Beneath the echoes of time, the stories of forgotten lives resurface.
Dive deeper into the archive to uncover the fragments of what was once lost.
Each tale awaits its rediscovery.

· Cities began as beacons of technological progress and prosperity. Machines hummed and human innovation seemed to herald a bright future. But in the pursuit of progress, city leaders ignored the fragility of nature. Wilderness is replaced by concrete, forests by factories, and animals that once lived in harmony with humans are pushed to the edge. As technology advances, it promises to solve problems but only exacerbates them-pollution, environmental collapse, and the erosion of empathy. In the shadow of this rapid urban sprawl, the first whispers of the forgotten ones begin. Animals that once roamed free now find themselves displaced, discarded or imprisoned.

· An initiative called Project Cleanup was launched to eradicate stray animals and “unwanted” creatures that have become a burden on the urban landscape. In the name of progress, society pursued a vision of order and cleanliness - a sterilized future where nature is carefully managed, controlled and planned. With the elimination of these “problems,” it is said, humanity can finally live in harmony with the world. But in reality, something more sinister has taken root. The lifeless landscape is slowly becoming a series of empty, sterile spaces. Animals - once part of the human world - disappeared, either banished or erased. Humanity's connection to the natural world is broken beyond repair.

· In 3055, the Final Exile program was officially implemented.

· Those few creatures that managed to survive the Cleanup Plan were forcibly expelled from human civilization and lived in forgotten corners of the world. These desolate, remote and dangerous places became the last refuge of endangered species. Far from their former homes, humans sought salvation from the wider world. But the Earth - once full of life and possibility - has become an empty shell.
